Neuroplasticity & Growth-mindset

What is Neuroplasticity?

Neuroplasticity is the ability of neural networks in the brain to grow and change through repetition of habits and continuous review of information.

How does this become helpful?

Understanding how our brain learns and being conscious of it will maximize our brain's performance and essentially our learning performance. Repetition is the key to building neuroplasticity. The more we practice something or the more we review information - the more neuron pathways are created forming stronger connections making the task we're practicing or info were learning become like second nature to us.

Whats my own experience with neuroplasticity?

I've had some experience with it not knowing about it or without knowing that was even a thing? But it definitely works and from my own experiences I know this to be true! It happens but we don't really notice it as an actually thing - in laymans terms we call it 'you got better at it' or 'experience'. In the brief research I completed this is actually neuroplasticity.

Ever remember playing a game that you really enjoyed in your childhood and when you start playing it, its a little hard figuring out how the game works and how to play. But the longer you played, and the more often you played the better you got at it? Thats neuroplasticity! And did you notice when you haven't played for a while you get a little rusty at it? Thats neuroplasticity again!

The more you practice something the more neural pathways are formed in your brain, the less you practice something the more neural pathways are pruned!

What is the Growth-mindset?

The growth mindset is the belief that one's abilities/qualities, specifically intelligence, can be developed.

How does this become helpful?

This is empowering for individuals to understand that they can become better at something rather than having been born with a limited or fixed amount of intelligence/skills.

Whats my own experience with the Growth-mindset?

I read Carol Dweck's Mindset book last year (2021) and it helped me to understand how I had a fixed mindset and how it came about. It damaged my learning experience growing up in school and essentially helped me believe that I wasn't capable of learning or thriving at school.

Learning about the fixed mindset and growth mindset helped me understand that difficulty learning something doesn't imply that I'm not capable or intelligent but rather a challenge that will help me grow and that in fact, challenges are completely normal and everyone experiences them.

So as I continue my learning journey not only in the Dev Academy but also in life, I will keep building a growth mindset and approach learning with the growth mindset to keep developing myself and to continue discovering the best version of myself.
