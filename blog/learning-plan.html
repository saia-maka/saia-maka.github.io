My learning plan

Saia's learning plan for Mataī Dev Academy 2022

My long term goal is to become a software engineer and to run a business developing/distributing software and web applications.

I like to think that I keep an open mind with most things in life. I'm always open to feedback and I'm open to different points of views which I believe helps me adjust to different environments, different personalities, learning new things or adopting new beliefs, and also helps to challenge my own knowledge and beliefs. I'm also quite a humble person which I feel is a really great strength but sometimes I know I deflect praise and as I've learnt already in foundations is that I need to accept it as it will help build my confidence. I have a great work ethic and can persevere during hard times. To assist that I need to work on organising myself more and not getting distracted so that I can get the most out of my learning and bring the best out of myself.

As I have mentioned I struggle to reach out for help, however I will commit myself to growing out of this habit/behaviour. When I need help or when I don't understand something I will put my hand up or I will ask for help from other students/facilitators in a timely way (after I've had a decent go myself), usually on discord or otherwise in person.

I would love to develop my organisation skills as I believe it is key to maintaining success in learning, work and other areas of my life. I would also love to develop my shyness/openness to others. I struggle to reach out to people who can help, especially creating conversation with others and putting myself out there, and how to interact with others in a social setting. This is a huge piece that I feel I've been missing and struggling with for a long time so I would really appreciate working on this. I would also love to really step outside my comfort zone to work on my leadership skills - practice mentoring and being mentored, helping others learn and to practice taking a leadership role. Finally, I would love to work on my teamworking skills as working in a team is essential to working as a developer and set me up for success in different teams in the future.

It is my hope the facilitators can continue to help me adjust and prime myself to reach my goals, to set myself up for success in terms on staying on the right track with my study/work and to get the most out of this learning experience. Whether it be in the form of feedback, a casual conversation and or extra learning material they believe or can see is relevant to what I need, I'm more than happy to take it on board. I'll take any help I can get and will keep an open-mind.

I do have whanau commitments as I'm a father but they are usually taken care of outside of my working hours or by my partner. If for some reason I need to take care of something or need to be excused I will let the appropriate people know as soon as I can.

I will schedule specific times for myself for when I'm working and when I'm not. For example, times I will start for the day, times I will finish, and days I will be working. My family around me will be made aware and reminded of this commitment to help support and prime myself and my environment to enable me to work productively, safely and to the best of my ability. If and when I can help other students, I will make a decent effort to do so. If and when I need assistance from or to report to facilitators, I will also make a decent effort to do so. I will not put the safety of others at risk, I will treat others with respect and do my best to put my best foot forward at every stage of my learning.

I will take brief moments to breath and calm myself to manage lesser challenging intensities of emotions or stress. For more challenging intensities I will take a timeout to step away, dust myself off, get some air into me, talk to a facilitator or call a support person or two, then return and communicate how I was feeling and why I had to step away. Otherwise, I will communicate to my facilitators how Im feeling and seek advice/assistance where appropriate.
