Problem solving

How do I solve a problem?

Some might say 'you just solve it and get on with it?', I said the same thing in the past. Now having had more life experience I'd say the first step to solving a problem is knowing what the problem actually is? And like any problem, it can be broken down into smaller problems that collectively fixed, will solve the original problem you had.

Name a problem that I had?

A problem that I had recently was when I was working with some data that essentially was a grade book containing the names and grade scores of 4 students. I had to write a program that would find the grade scores of any student of choice and calculate an average of their scores.

What happened next?

I jumped straight into it and wrote some code that flowed off the top of my brain and ran it through the machine. It didn't work so I played with the code for a while until I realized I had a problem and I was stuck! The first step I took to solve this was to figure out what my problem was: At first it was that I wasn't able to access the student's scores. When I used the rubber ducky technique (telling & talking to myself about what I was trying to do/doing) and dived deeper into my problem I quickly realized I had more problems: I could access the student's score but the code I wrote was wrong (obviously), I didn't know for sure what I was doing anymore and I couldn't tell which stages of my code were broken.

So how did I solve the problem then?

Next, I jumped onto google and ran a couple of searches to read different codes to figure out how to actually use the code and to learn how to to access the data I couldn't access before. Then I had to write out some steps to follow (psuedocode) so that I could figure out where I was going with my code again and what I was doing. Finally, I used console logs (alert messages) to see which parts of my code weren't working. Now that I had a plan of how to approach the problem and had found the appropriate tools & knowledge to attack back with, I quickly got back on track, made a few adjustments, and got through the challenge!

How did it feel and what did I learn?

I did feel a little frustrated initially and especially when I started to lose track of what I was actually trying to do and when I couldn't the data I needed. Like most situations you get familiar with, you don't panic and sort of just know you'll get through it. I knew I just had to take it slow and find the right way to approach the challenge. It felt good getting through and felt good to see my problem-solving skills improving.
