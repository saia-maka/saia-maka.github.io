My identity & values

Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and the consequences of your action?

During my time on Take2, I and another student had the pleasure of planning a family day for our class that the CEO could pitch to get approved. While I'm sure the two of us could've come up with something spectacular, I knew we could come up with something even better if we included the entire class. So instead of leaving it to the two of us (Class Leaders), which we could've easily done, we planned everything together as a class. Some feedback we got afterwards was that it was a execellent plan. In the end this developed the class bond and culture as it created a sense of inclusiveness and responsibility for all.

Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity?

Discipline is a big part of my culture as a Tongan and I feel it strongly influenced values such a respect, humility and kindness. Any sort of signs of disrespect is heavily frowned upon and sometimes even met with physical discipline (considered on a case-by-case basis). Growing up in this culture helped me to be humble and to respect others, especially my parents, elders and others. It also taught me to be kind to others. It wasn't always pleasant as I was a difficult kid but I there were also many positives in the end.

Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development?

I think my strengths will help me to be a great student in the dev academy in terms of how I conduct myself and my approach towards learning. My limitations on the other hand, if I don't pay attention, will limit the growth and learning experience I will have throughout dev academy and in my career. Improving those limitations will help me develop as a student, a professional and as a person exponentially.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why?

During Take2 there was some feedback work we had to complete as part of a design workshop we did with Xero, our facilitator at the time asked some students in our class if they could re do their work as it was not up to a satisfactory standard to be sent through to Xero. The facilitator asked me to help some of the others since I had already completed mine. I felt some tension/resistance working with some of the students and was initially difficult working together. My strategy was to help them see the work in a different way, that it wasn't just any task we had to complete, but a opportunity to build rapport and that it would be a chance to start building their own reputation within the tech sector. It helped most take it more seriously but not all so next time i'd try being more patient and ask more questions to help build with better/improved strategies.
